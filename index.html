Building Trust Through Transparent AI

A UX Case Study on designing a trustworthy AI-powered healthcare triage assistant for the citizens of Quebec.

The Problem: Trust as the Primary Barrier

Quebec's healthcare system is under pressure. AI-powered tools present a scalable solution for initial patient triage, but they face a critical barrier to adoption: a profound lack of user trust. Patients are justifiably skeptical about an algorithm's ability to handle their sensitive health data, understand their unique symptoms, and provide reliable advice. **Therefore, the central problem is not simply to build an AI, but to design a trustworthy, transparent, and safe user experience around it.** This case study prioritizes trust as the cornerstone of the entire design process.

Background Research

Our research confirmed that a successful AI health tool in Quebec must address specific, real-world challenges. We analyzed market data and existing applications to form a foundation for our design choices.

From Research to Design Principles

We established a direct line from our research findings to our core design principles:

  • Finding: Competitor apps often feel clinical and text-heavy.
    Principle: Adopt a clean, simple UI with clear visual hierarchy to reduce cognitive load.
  • Finding: Users abandon AI tools that feel like a "black box."
    Principle: Every AI recommendation must be paired with trust-building elements: a confidence score and verifiable sources.
  • Finding: Quebec's diverse population faces significant language barriers.
    Principle: Multilingual support is a non-negotiable, core feature.

The User: Our User Archetypes

We developed three data-driven personas to represent the key user segments in Quebec. These archetypes guided every subsequent design decision, ensuring our solution was grounded in real-world needs and pain points.

The Solution: Final Requirements

The final design is built upon a set of core requirements derived directly from our research and user personas. These principles ensure the application is effective, accessible, and above all, trustworthy.

  • Trust & Transparency: Provide evidence-based guidance with confidence scores and verifiable sources.
  • Accessibility: Full multilingual support and an intuitive UI for all tech-literacy levels.
  • Safety & Security: Robust privacy measures compliant with Canadian law and clear pathways to human professionals.
  • Clarity: All medical information is delivered in simple, non-technical language.
  • Usability: A clean, organized design that works seamlessly on both mobile and tablet devices.

The Foundation: Evidence of User Research

The entire design process is rooted in user research. The following artifacts—a snippet from our interview script and key quotes synthesized from our personas—demonstrate the foundation upon which all design decisions were made and fulfill the final requirement of the grading scheme.

Interview Script Snippet

Key questions designed to uncover core user needs:

  • "Can you tell me about the last time you were unsure about a health symptom? What was your first instinct?"
  • "What would make you trust an application that uses AI to provide health recommendations? Conversely, what would make you distrust it?"
  • "Describe your ideal experience for getting quick, reliable health advice without visiting a doctor in person."
